Two wheels, a carbon frame and a whole lot of graft. That sums up my 2020.


For years now I have only ever been a commuter when it comes to cycling, some could say 150 miles a week isn't your average cycle commute, however for me I just saw it as travelling to and from central london for work.


Fast forward to 2020 and with the closure of gyms I purchased my first first road bike as a way to keep fit, with this my usual means of commute turned in to very enjoyable hobby.


What started as just just me and a friend going out on rides together, quickly turned in to a group of like minded people.


At the start of may I joined a few of the monthly challenges, with the main one being the 'May cycling distance challenge', could I really travel 1,250km in a month by bike?


You best believe I could, eventually totalling 1,273km for the month with a day to spare, just me, my bike and a whole lot of graft I got the challenge done.


What started the month was a London football stadium tour ride, ending with a round trip of London to Brighton and a whopping 166km's. If I had to choose a favourite ride of the month that would be it closely followed by London to Southend, although the hardest being London to Cambridge, a very hilly ride.


As the summer days and lockdown came to and end, unfortunately the regularity of the rides weren't as frequent, however as we are now in February 2021 with winter coming to an end, you know cycling season is about to begin.


Hopefully 2021 is a better year for us and we can get back to some form of normality along with travel restrictions being eased, if the latter is achieved then my goal for the year will be to cycle from London to Paris to the iconic Eiffel Tower.

Can I do that in the space of 24 hours? You bet I can.


Here's to the year, cycle season is upon us.
